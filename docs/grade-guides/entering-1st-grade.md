# Entering 1st Grade Grade Guide

Children entering 1st grade are moving from early exploration toward more fluent reading, writing, and number sense. Summer work should protect confidence while strengthening foundational skills.

## Reading and Language

- Know most letter names and common letter sounds.
- Blend and segment simple consonant-vowel-consonant words.
- Read common high-frequency words from Kindergarten.
- Listen to a story and describe characters, setting, and major events.
- Ask and answer questions about read-alouds and simple nonfiction.

## Writing

- Write first and last name.
- Write uppercase and lowercase letters with improving formation.
- Draw and write to tell about a personal experience.
- Use spaces between words with adult support.
- Begin sentences with capital letters and end with punctuation.

## Math

- Count forward to 100 by ones and tens.
- Read, write, and compare numbers to 20.
- Add and subtract within 10 using objects, drawings, fingers, or mental strategies.
- Understand teen numbers as ten ones and extra ones.
- Measure and compare length, weight, and capacity informally.
- Identify two-dimensional and three-dimensional shapes.

## Science and Social Studies

- Observe patterns in weather, plants, animals, light, and sound.
- Describe needs of living things.
- Understand family, school, neighborhood, maps, rules, and responsibilities.

## Summer Practice Ideas

- Read decodable books and reread favorites for fluency.
- Write one sentence about the day a few times per week.
- Play addition and subtraction games with dice, cards, or snacks.
- Count collections and group items into tens and ones.

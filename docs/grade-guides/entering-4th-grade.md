# Entering 4th Grade Grade Guide

Children entering 4th grade benefit from strengthening multiplication, division, reading stamina, and clear paragraph writing before the school year begins.

## Reading and Language

- Read fiction and nonfiction for sustained periods.
- Summarize using main idea, theme, key details, and text evidence.
- Compare characters, settings, events, accounts, and points of view.
- Use academic vocabulary and reference tools.

## Writing

- Write multi-paragraph pieces with a clear topic, details, and conclusion.
- Use transition words to show sequence, contrast, cause, and effect.
- Support opinions with reasons and examples.
- Edit for grammar, punctuation, spelling, and sentence variety.

## Math

- Know multiplication and division facts through 10.
- Multiply and divide within 100 using strategies and fact fluency.
- Understand place value through thousands and beyond.
- Add and subtract multi-digit numbers accurately.
- Work with fractions as numbers and compare simple fractions.
- Measure area and perimeter.

## Science and Social Studies

- Explore energy, motion, ecosystems, Earth features, weathering, and scientific investigation.
- Study regions, maps, cultures, government, economics, and historical events.

## Summer Practice Ideas

- Read a mix of novels, biographies, and nonfiction articles.
- Keep a response journal with summaries and opinions.
- Practice multiplication facts through games and real-world arrays.
- Plan small projects using budgets, measurements, maps, or data.

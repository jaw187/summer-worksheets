# Entering 5th Grade Grade Guide

Children entering 5th grade are preparing for more complex texts, multi-step math, research, and independent organization. Summer practice should include reading, writing, math fluency, and project-based thinking.

## Reading and Language

- Read longer fiction, nonfiction, poetry, and primary-source excerpts.
- Analyze theme, structure, author's purpose, point of view, and evidence.
- Compare multiple texts on the same topic.
- Use morphology, context, and reference tools to learn vocabulary.

## Writing

- Write organized multi-paragraph essays, narratives, and reports.
- Use evidence, examples, quotations, and explanations.
- Plan, draft, revise, edit, and publish with increasing independence.
- Use correct grammar, punctuation, capitalization, and spelling in polished work.

## Math

- Multiply multi-digit whole numbers.
- Divide using place-value strategies and standard algorithms as appropriate.
- Add, subtract, multiply, and compare fractions.
- Understand decimals to hundredths and thousandths.
- Convert measurement units within a system.
- Solve multi-step word problems using diagrams, equations, or tables.

## Science and Social Studies

- Investigate matter, ecosystems, Earth systems, space, engineering design, and data.
- Study geography, early American history, government, economics, and civic participation.

## Summer Practice Ideas

- Read a longer book series or nonfiction topic set.
- Write reviews, arguments, experiment notes, or research summaries.
- Practice multiplication, division, fractions, and decimals in real contexts.
- Build a small project that requires planning, measurement, revision, and explanation.

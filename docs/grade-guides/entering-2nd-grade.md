# Entering 2nd Grade Grade Guide

Children entering 2nd grade are expected to grow into more independent readers, writers, and problem solvers. Summer practice should include short daily reading and regular number work.

## Reading and Language

- Read grade-appropriate texts with accuracy and expression.
- Use phonics patterns such as blends, digraphs, silent e, vowel teams, and common endings.
- Retell stories and identify lesson, problem, solution, characters, and setting.
- Use text features in nonfiction, including headings, captions, and diagrams.
- Build vocabulary through reading and conversation.

## Writing

- Write several related sentences on one topic.
- Use capitals, punctuation, and spacing more consistently.
- Spell common sight words and phonics-based words.
- Write opinions, narratives, and informative pieces with adult support.

## Math

- Add and subtract within 20 fluently.
- Understand place value for ones, tens, and hundreds.
- Add and subtract two-digit numbers using drawings or strategies.
- Tell time to the hour and half-hour.
- Measure length with standard and nonstandard units.
- Solve simple word problems.

## Science and Social Studies

- Compare properties of materials.
- Observe plant and animal life cycles.
- Study communities, maps, timelines, goods, services, and civic responsibilities.

## Summer Practice Ideas

- Read for 15 to 20 minutes most days.
- Keep a simple journal with two or three sentences per entry.
- Practice math facts with games instead of timed pressure.
- Use coins, clocks, calendars, and measuring tools in real situations.

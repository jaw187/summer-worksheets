# Entering Kindergarten Grade Guide

Children entering Kindergarten are usually building comfort with routines, early literacy, early math, and independence. Summer practice should feel playful, short, and hands-on.

## Reading and Language

- Recognize and name many uppercase and lowercase letters.
- Hear and produce rhyming words.
- Notice beginning sounds in familiar words.
- Understand that print moves left to right and top to bottom.
- Retell a simple story with a beginning, middle, and end.
- Speak in complete thoughts and answer simple who, what, where, and why questions.

## Writing and Fine Motor

- Hold crayons, pencils, and scissors with growing control.
- Trace lines, curves, letters, and simple shapes.
- Write first name with mostly recognizable letters.
- Draw a picture and tell an adult about it.
- Practice coloring, cutting, gluing, and folding.

## Math

- Count objects to 10 or more with one-to-one matching.
- Recognize numerals 0 through 10.
- Compare groups using more, fewer, and same.
- Sort objects by color, shape, size, or type.
- Name basic shapes: circle, square, triangle, rectangle.
- Notice and extend simple patterns.

## Science and Social Studies

- Observe weather, plants, animals, and seasonal changes.
- Use describing words such as big, small, wet, dry, hot, cold, soft, and rough.
- Understand basic personal information: name, age, family members, and community helpers.
- Practice classroom habits such as taking turns, listening, cleaning up, and asking for help.

## Summer Practice Ideas

- Read aloud daily and ask the child to point out letters or retell favorite parts.
- Count snacks, steps, toys, or items in a grocery cart.
- Sort laundry, blocks, buttons, or leaves.
- Draw after an outing and dictate one sentence about the picture.
- Play short listening games: "What starts with /m/?" or "Tell me two words that rhyme."

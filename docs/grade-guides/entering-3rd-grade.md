# Entering 3rd Grade Grade Guide

Children entering 3rd grade are crossing into more independent learning. Reading comprehension, organized writing, multiplication readiness, and stamina become increasingly important.

## Reading and Language

- Read chapter books and informational texts with growing independence.
- Identify main idea, key details, character traits, point of view, and text evidence.
- Use context clues, prefixes, suffixes, and root words to understand vocabulary.
- Discuss books using complete answers and examples from the text.

## Writing

- Plan and write paragraphs with a topic sentence and supporting details.
- Write narratives with sequence and descriptive detail.
- Write opinion and informative pieces with reasons or facts.
- Revise for clarity and edit for capitals, punctuation, and spelling.

## Math

- Add and subtract within 1000 using place-value strategies.
- Build fluency with addition and subtraction facts.
- Understand arrays, equal groups, repeated addition, and early multiplication.
- Solve one- and two-step word problems.
- Tell time to the nearest five minutes and solve elapsed-time problems.
- Understand halves, thirds, and fourths as equal parts.

## Science and Social Studies

- Investigate life cycles, habitats, weather, forces, and simple systems.
- Study geography, communities, government, economics, and local history.

## Summer Practice Ideas

- Read longer books and talk about evidence for answers.
- Write short paragraphs about trips, books, experiments, or opinions.
- Practice skip-counting by 2s, 3s, 4s, 5s, and 10s.
- Cook, build, measure, and compare data from real activities.
